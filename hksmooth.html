Introduction

Eigenfunctions of the Laplace-Beltrami Operator

The Laplace-Beltrami Operator \(\Delta\) corresponding to the surface parameterization \(p=X\left(u^{1}, u^{2}\right)\) is defined as

\[ \Delta=\frac{1}{\operatorname{det} g^{1 / 2}} \sum_{i, j=1}^{2} \frac{\partial}{\partial u^{i}}\left(\operatorname{det} g^{1 / 2} g^{i j} \frac{\partial}{\partial u^{j}}\right) \]

where \(g=(g_{ij})\) is the Riemannian metric tensor. Example: Laplacian in spherical coordinates

Consider the eigen-problem

\[ \Delta \phi_i=-\lambda_i\phi_i, \quad \lambda_i\geq 0 \]

Sometimes we will impose some boundary conditions (mostly \(\phi_i=0\) on \(\partial \Omega\)) to guarantee orthonormality of the solution.

Heat Kernel Smoothing

Heat Kernel

The heat kernel on Riemannian manifold \(M\) is a function \(K(x,y,t)\) (or \(K_{t}(x,y)\), also written \(e^{-t\Delta}\)) on \(M\times M\times \mathbb{R}_+\) such that [5,6]

\[ \begin{cases} &\dfrac{\partial K_t(x,y)}{\partial t} = \Delta K_t(x,y) \\ & K_0(x,y)=\delta(x-y) \end{cases}. \]

This also means that the convolution \(K_{t}*f(x):=\int_{M} K(x, y, t) f(y) \mathrm{d} y \) is the unique solution of the heat equation [1,5]

\[ \begin{cases} &\dfrac{\partial v(x,t)}{\partial t} = \Delta v(x,t) \\ & v(x,t=0)=f(x) \end{cases}. \]

The formal expression for Heat kernel is [7]

\[ K(t,x,y)=\sum_{i=0}^{\infty} e^{-\lambda_i t}\phi_i(x)\phi_i(y),\quad x,y\in M,\; t\in \mathbb{R}_+ \]

where \(\{\phi_i\}\) is an orthonormal basis of \(L^2(M)\) that satisfies \(\Delta \phi_i=\lambda_i\phi_i\). To validate this statement, put into the convolution formula, use \(f=\sum a_i\omega_i\) to get the same result as \(K_tf=\sum a_i\exp(-\lambda_i t)\omega_i\).
