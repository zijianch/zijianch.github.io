Gradient Descent with Kantorovich's dual formulation

Recall Kantorovich's dual formulation:

\[ \begin{aligned} & \inf _{\phi, \psi} \bigg\{\int_X \phi(x) \mathrm d \mu(x)+\int_Y \psi(y) \mathrm d \nu(y) \bigg\}\\ \text { s.t. } & \phi(x)+\psi(y) \geq\langle x, y\rangle \quad \forall x \in X \text { and } y \in Y \end{aligned} \]

If \(c(x,y)=\frac{1}{2}\|x-y\|^2\) , then using \(\|x-y\|^2=\|x\|^2+\|y\|^2-2\langle x,y\rangle\), we have

\[ u(x)+v(y) \leq \frac{1}{2}\|x-y\|^2 \Longleftrightarrow \langle x, y\rangle \leq \underbrace{\frac{1}{2}\|x\|^2-u(x)}_{\phi(x)}+\underbrace{\frac{1}{2}\|y\|^2-v(y)}_{\psi(y)} \]

As a consequence, the problem becomes

\[ \begin{aligned} & \inf _{\phi, \psi} \bigg\{\int_X \phi(x) \mathrm d \mu(x)+\int_Y \psi(y) \mathrm d \nu(y) \bigg\}\\ \text { s.t. } & \phi(x)+\psi(y) \geq\langle x, y\rangle \quad \forall x \in X \text { and } y \in Y \end{aligned} \]

One important fact: the optimal pair \((\phi,\psi)\) are convex conjugate such that

\[ \begin{cases} \psi(y)&=\sup\limits_{x \in X}(\langle x, y\rangle-\phi(x)) \\ \phi(x)&=\sup\limits_{y \in Y}(\langle x, y\rangle-\psi(y)) \end{cases} \]
